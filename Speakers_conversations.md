# Two-Speaker Conversation: "The Day After"

**Speaker 1:** Moses (African, recounting yesterday's kayaking trip and dinner)
**Speaker 2:** Tarrah (American, took him kayaking the day before)

*Setting: A quiet coffee shop, the next afternoon. They're sitting across from each other, recapping yesterday.*

---

**Speaker 2:** Hey. Sorry, sorry, am I late? I had to park like three blocks away.

**Speaker 1:** No, no, you're fine, I just got here myself, like, two minutes ago.

**Speaker 2:** Okay good. (pause) How are you feeling? Like, today.

**Speaker 1:** Honestly? I'm still — I'm still feeling it. Like, my arms.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Yeah?

**Speaker 1:** Yeah, my arms are like... it's a different kind of sore, you know? Not gym sore. It's like a — a deep, deep sore.

**Speaker 2:** That's the paddling. That's, like, all rotator cuff and back. People don't expect that.

**Speaker 1:** No, I did not expect that. I thought, okay, it's a boat, you just sit there and—

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) And just float.

**Speaker 1:** Exactly! Just float! But no, no, it was — it was work.

**Speaker 2:** Well, I mean, you did good though. Like, genuinely. For your first time, you didn't tip us once.

**Speaker 1:** I almost did. That one part, by the — what was it, the rocks? Near the bend?

**Speaker 2:** Oh my god, yes. When you leaned the wrong way?

**Speaker 1:** I leaned the wrong way and you just went "Kofi. KOFI." (laughs)

**Speaker 2:** I panicked! I thought we were going in.

**Speaker 1:** We were so close to going in.

**Speaker 2:** We were so close. But, okay, but other than that, I think it went really well. Like, the weather was—

**Speaker 1:** The weather was perfect.

**Speaker 2:** Right? That light in the evening, when the sun was kind of low—

**Speaker 1:** Mm-hm.

**Speaker 2:** —over the water, that was just... yeah. That's why I wanted to take you there. That spot specifically.

**Speaker 1:** It was beautiful. I took, uh, I think I took like thirty photos.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Thirty?

**Speaker 1:** At least. My phone was like "storage almost full" and I was like, no, not now, not now.

**Speaker 2:** Did any of them turn out good?

**Speaker 1:** A few, yeah. I'll send them to you. There's one of you, actually, mid-paddle, and you're laughing at something—

**Speaker 2:** Oh no.

**Speaker 1:** No, no, it's a good one! You look, um — you look really happy in it.

**Speaker 2:** (pause) Okay, well, send it to me, I wanna see.

**Speaker 1:** I will, I will. Tonight, probably, when I'm home.

**Speaker 2:** Oh — and remember that heron? The big one, on the log?

**Speaker 1:** Oh my gosh, yes! I almost dropped my paddle trying to get a picture of it.

**Speaker 2:** You did drop it, a little, you fumbled it.

**Speaker 1:** I did not drop it, I just — repositioned it.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Sure. "Repositioned."

**Speaker 1:** It startled me, okay? It just took off right when I was reaching for my phone.

**Speaker 2:** That whole stretch of the river is good for birds, actually. I've seen herons, ducks, one time I think I saw an osprey but I'm not totally sure.

**Speaker 1:** An osprey, really? That's — that's a big bird.

**Speaker 2:** Yeah, it was way up though, so honestly it coulda been a hawk, I don't know my birds that well.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) Confident guess though.

**Speaker 2:** I commit to my guesses.

**Speaker 1:** I noticed. (pause) So, okay — after, when we got back to the dock, and we were just like, dripping, exhausted—

**Speaker 2:** Exhausted. I sat in that car and I did not want to move.

**Speaker 1:** I just sat there for like two minutes. I think you were already, like, halfway through changing your shoes and I was just sitting.

**Speaker 2:** I was hungry, that's why. I was like, okay, we need food, like, now.

**Speaker 1:** That's true, you did say that. You said it like three times actually.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Did I?

**Speaker 1:** "I'm starving, I'm starving." Yes.

**Speaker 2:** Okay, fair, fair. But — okay, that place you picked though, that was a good call. That little Italian spot?

**Speaker 1:** Oh, yeah, that one's actually — I go there sometimes. With, um, with my cousin, when he's in town.

**Speaker 2:** I didn't know that. I just assumed you picked it off, like, Google or something.

**Speaker 1:** No, no, I know the owner a little. He's the one who, uh, brought us that bread, the warm one—

**Speaker 2:** Oh my gosh, the bread.

**Speaker 1:** —with the oil and the, the balsamic—

**Speaker 2:** I could've just eaten that and gone home, honestly. I was so full just off the bread.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) But then you still ate the whole plate of pasta.

**Speaker 2:** I know! I don't — I don't know how. I just kept going.

**Speaker 1:** It was the mushroom one, right? The one you got?

**Speaker 2:** Yeah, the mushroom risotto. What'd you get again, the, um—

**Speaker 1:** The seafood linguine.

**Speaker 2:** Right, right. How was it?

**Speaker 1:** It was good. A little — actually a little too much garlic for me, but, it was good.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) You said that like four times during dinner too. "Too much garlic."

**Speaker 1:** Did I? I don't remember saying it that much.

**Speaker 2:** You did. It's okay, I believe you, it's a lot of garlic.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) Okay, okay, fair. Um, but the conversation though, that was — I really liked that. Just, you know, talking. Properly. Not rushing.

**Speaker 2:** Yeah, no, same. I feel like normally on, like, first... I don't know if I'd even call it a date, but—

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) I would call it a date.

**Speaker 2:** Okay — okay, fine, on a first date, usually it's kind of awkward, there's like, gaps, you know, where nobody knows what to say.

**Speaker 1:** Mm.

**Speaker 2:** But that didn't really happen with us. Like, we just kept finding stuff to talk about.

**Speaker 1:** Yeah, I noticed that too. Even the — remember when we got onto, uh, your brother? And his whole thing with moving to Denver?

**Speaker 2:** Oh my god, yes, I went off on a whole tangent about that.

**Speaker 1:** It was a good tangent though. I liked hearing about your family. And then you asked about mine, and I just — I don't usually talk about home that much, but it felt easy, somehow.

**Speaker 2:** (softly) Yeah. I liked that part a lot, actually.

**Speaker 1:** (pause) Me too.

**Speaker 2:** Like, what was that story you told, about your dad and the — was it a goat? Did I hear that right?

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) Yes, the goat story, yes. I can't believe you remember that.

**Speaker 2:** It was a good story! Tell it again, I wanna make sure I have it right for, like, when I retell it to my friends.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) Okay, okay — so, basically, my father, back when I was maybe ten, he bought this goat for, I think, a wedding, a relative's wedding—

**Speaker 2:** Right, right.

**Speaker 1:** —and the goat got loose, like, two days before, and the whole neighborhood was looking for it—

**Speaker 2:** And your dad found it in, like, somebody's garden, eating their—

**Speaker 1:** Their cabbages! Yes! Okay, you do remember.

**Speaker 2:** I told you, good story.

**Speaker 1:** It's a stupid story.

**Speaker 2:** It's a great story, don't do that, don't undersell it.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) Okay, fine, fine. It's a great story.

**Speaker 2:** Thank you. So, okay, also — dessert. We should talk about dessert, because that tiramisu—

**Speaker 1:** Oh, the tiramisu, yes.

**Speaker 2:** I think about it sometimes, like, randomly, during the day. That's how good it was.

**Speaker 1:** I'm glad I got the extra fork, then. Otherwise you would have just taken mine.

**Speaker 2:** I absolutely would have. No shame.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) None at all, I noticed.

**Speaker 2:** So how long was the drive back to my place after? Like twenty minutes?

**Speaker 1:** Something like that. Maybe twenty-five, with the traffic by the bridge.

**Speaker 2:** Right, right, there was that backup.

**Speaker 1:** Yeah. But it was, um — it was a nice drive. You had that playlist going.

**Speaker 2:** Oh — yeah, my "chill" playlist. Did you like it?

**Speaker 1:** I did, actually. There was that one song, the — the slower one, I don't know the name, but I kind of want it now.

**Speaker 2:** I'll send it to you. I'll send you the whole playlist, honestly.

**Speaker 1:** Please do.

**Speaker 2:** And then you walked me to the door, which — you didn't have to do that, by the way, but I appreciated it.

**Speaker 1:** Of course I did. I wasn't just gonna drop you at the curb. That's not — no.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Some guys would.

**Speaker 1:** Not me.

**Speaker 2:** Yeah, I noticed. (small pause) And then you headed home after that, right? You said it was, what, another forty minutes for you?

**Speaker 1:** About forty, yeah. Forty-five with the lights on Route 9.

**Speaker 2:** That's a long drive after a whole day like that. You weren't, like, falling asleep at the wheel or anything?

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) No, no, I had the windows down, some music going, I was fine. A little tired, but fine. Worth it.

**Speaker 2:** Good. I was gonna text and check but then I figured you'd just be sleeping by the time you got in.

**Speaker 1:** I was out the second I hit the bed, honestly. Didn't even, uh, didn't even change out of my clothes, I think.

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) That bad?

**Speaker 1:** That bad. Whole day just — caught up with me at once.

**Speaker 2:** Same, actually, same. I slept so well last night. Like, didn't even hear my alarm, my roommate had to knock on my door.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) That's how you know it was a good day.

**Speaker 2:** Exactly. Worth the consequences.

**Speaker 1:** (pause) So... we should do it again sometime. The kayaking, the — all of it.

**Speaker 2:** Yeah, I'd really like that. There's actually another spot, a little further north, with this — it's quieter, fewer people, I think you'd like it.

**Speaker 1:** Yeah? Tell me about it.

**Speaker 2:** It's, um, it's more like a lake, not a river, so the water's calmer, easier, honestly, probably easier on your arms.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) That sounds appealing right now.

**Speaker 2:** I figured. There's also a little place nearby that does, like, wood-fired pizza, right on the water. We could do that after instead.

**Speaker 1:** Now you're just describing my perfect day.

**Speaker 2:** I know my audience.

**Speaker 1:** Maybe not next weekend though — give my arms a little time—

**Speaker 2:** (laughs) Fair, fair, give your arms a break.

**Speaker 1:** But, yeah, soon. I'd like that a lot, actually.

**Speaker 2:** Me too. (pause) Okay, well — I should probably get going soon, actually, I told my mom I'd call her.

**Speaker 1:** Yeah, no, of course. This was — this was nice, though. Just sitting and talking about it.

**Speaker 2:** It really was. We should make this a thing, honestly — the day-after debrief.

**Speaker 1:** (laughs) I like that. The debrief.

**Speaker 2:** Official tradition now.

**Speaker 1:** Okay, deal.

*[End of conversation]*
